The problem

Dr. Lauren Okafor had built a solid two-location practice over eight years, but by mid-2024 her front desk team was drowning. After-hours calls were going to voicemail — and roughly 40% of them never got a callback before the patient called a competitor. Recall outreach for overdue cleanings was being done manually once a month, and the scheduling inbox had a 24–36 hour response lag. A new patient who filled out the "Request an Appointment" form might wait two days to hear back.

Hiring a second coordinator would have cost roughly $52,000/year in loaded labor — and still wouldn't have solved the after-hours gap.

What we built

1

After-hours voice agent — Week 1

Answers inbound calls when the office is closed. Collects patient info, routes urgencies as immediate SMS alerts to the on-call line. All other requests queued for front desk at 8 AM.

2

New patient intake agent — Week 2

Responds to website forms and missed-call texts within 90 seconds. Qualifies insurance, offers available slots from Dentrix, and books directly for accepted plans.

3

Recall outreach agent — Week 3

Runs nightly, identifies overdue patients, sends personalized text/email with a booking link. Stops automatically once booked. Escalates 18+ month inactive patients for a personal call.

The numbers

Metric Before After
After-hours call capture rate ~58% (40% never called back) 97%
New patient inquiry response time 24–36 hours Under 90 seconds
Recall appointments booked / month ~18 (manual, once/month) 41 (automated nightly)
Front desk hours on scheduling/recall ~22 hrs/week ~9 hrs/week (−59%)

Dollar-visible impact

$52,000/yr headcount avoided — second coordinator hire no longer needed
~$23,000/yr incremental recall revenue (23 additional hygiene visits/month)
$18–28K/yr new patient revenue uplift from faster response
Total monthly AI cost: $410/mo
"I was skeptical that a bot could handle dental scheduling — patients want to talk to a person. But what I found is that patients who text us at 9 PM don't want to wait until morning. They want an answer right now. The agent gives them that, and by the time my coordinator gets in, the appointment is already on the books. She's gone from putting out fires all morning to actually working on patient experience. That's the shift I've been trying to make for three years." — Dr. Lauren Okafor, DDS · Owner, Bright Smile Family Dentistry

The problem

Marcus Delgado had built one of the more productive mid-size buyer's agent teams in the South Bay. But they were losing deals before ever getting to the phone. Pulling leads from Zillow Premier Agent, Realtor.com, and Google, average first-response time was 2–4 hours. The data is brutal: 78% of buyers work with the first agent who contacts them.

"We were paying $12,000 a month in lead costs and then sleeping through the follow-up," Marcus said. "Nobody's sitting there refreshing the lead dashboard."

Beyond first response, a cold lead database of over 800 contacts sat completely untouched. Re-engagement was nonexistent.

What we built

1

Instant lead response agent — Week 1

Connects to Zillow, Realtor.com, and website forms via webhook. Responds to every new lead within 60–90 seconds, qualifies timeline and interest, books a 15-min call. Pushes everything to Follow Up Boss automatically.

2

Lead nurture + re-engagement agent — Week 2

30-day drip for unresponsive leads + separate sequence for 800-contact cold database. Personalized by lead source and timeline. Flags any response for immediate agent notification.

3

Appointment confirmation + no-show recovery — Week 3

Confirms 24 hrs and 2 hrs before scheduled calls. If a lead no-shows, sends rebooking within 10 minutes. No-show rate dropped from ~34% to under 12%.

The numbers

Metric Before After
Avg. inbound lead response time 2–4 hours Under 90 seconds
Lead-to-appointment conversion rate 11% 19% (+73%)
Cold database (800 contacts) re-engaged 0 — untouched 67 convos · 14 appts booked
Appointment no-show rate ~34% ~11% (−68%)
Agent hours/week on first response + follow-up ~18 hrs (across team) ~5 hrs (−72%)

Dollar-visible impact

$72,000/yr ISA headcount avoided
$110–160K/yr incremental GCI from improved lead conversion (8 additional closed transactions)
$40,500 direct GCI from cold database re-engagement in first 90 days
−43% effective cost-per-closed-deal on same $12K/mo lead budget
Total monthly AI cost: $590/mo
"I used to think the ISA model was the only way to compete at our lead volume. What I found out is that the ISA model exists because humans can't respond in 60 seconds — but the agent can. The leads don't know it's automated. They get a personal, relevant response in under two minutes and they feel taken care of. My agents are now only talking to people who are already warmed up and scheduled. That's the job I wanted them doing from the beginning." — Marcus Delgado · Team Lead, Harbor Peak Realty Group
