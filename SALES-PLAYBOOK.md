# Ramped AI — Sales Playbook v1

**Last updated:** 2026-04-29
**Audience:** Founder / sales caller — use on discovery calls, qualification chats, and inbound demos.

The script below is a **scaffold, not a recital**. Adapt the language to the prospect's tone — what's listed here is the *shape* of the conversation Jon runs, not a teleprompter.

---

## OPENER

> "How are you currently using AI in your business?"

[Let them answer.]

> "Got it. So what we do is a little different from what you're probably used to. Most people are using AI as a tool — you ask it something, it helps you, session ends. What we build is closer to a team. Agents that are running your operations 24/7, whether you're in a meeting, on a flight, or asleep. And they get better at your business every single week without you having to do anything."

---

## THE CORE PITCH

> "Here's the way I like to explain it. The AI tools most people are using right now — ChatGPT, Claude, whatever — are basically really smart chat windows. They're useful. But they reset. Every session you're starting over. The agent has no real memory of your business, no role, no workflows. You're the one holding it all together.
>
> What we install is different. Each agent has its own brain — a live knowledge base that updates as it works. It learns from every interaction. It refines its own playbooks based on what worked and what didn't. So on day one it's useful. By day 90 it's better than most human VAs because it has 90 days of compounded, specific context about your business that a human would've forgotten by month two.
>
> And it doesn't stop when you do. It's running your operations while you're focused on the stuff only you can do."

---

## MAKE IT CONCRETE

> "Here's what that actually looks like in practice. One of our clients — before working with us, every task had to go through them first. Even the automated ones needed babysitting. Now they have agents handling their client-facing work around the clock. When something breaks in a workflow, one agent flags another. They solve it and get back to work. Our client is completely out of the loop unless it actually needs them.
>
> That's the difference between **running a business** and **being trapped inside one**."

---

## URGENCY

> "The window is real. Right now, having agents running your operations is a genuine competitive advantage — most businesses don't have this. Twelve months from now, every smart business will. The people moving today are building a lead that compounds. The people who wait are playing catch-up against competitors who've had agents learning and improving for a year."

---

## THE OFFER

> "What we do at Ramped is handle all of it for you. The infrastructure, the memory architecture, the workflows — that's our job. You spend a few hours with us upfront so we understand your business, and we build agents specific to how you actually operate. Most clients free up 10–15 hours a week in the first month. By month three, the agents know their role better than a new hire would.
>
> Best next step is a quick workflow audit — 20 minutes, I'll show you exactly what your operations would look like with agents running them. No pressure, just an honest look. Does that work?"

---

## OBJECTION HANDLING

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| **"I'm already using AI and it's working."** | "The question isn't whether it works, it's whether it scales. Most people hit the ceiling right when their business needs it not to have one." |
| **"Sounds expensive."** | "Compared to a VA at $3–5k a month who you train, manage, and who forgets things — our agents run 24/7 and get better every week. Most clients are net positive within 60 days." |
| **"I want to build it myself."** | "The infrastructure and memory architecture alone usually takes 3–6 months to get right. You're getting a proven system from day one." |
| **"I don't have time."** | "You don't need to. That's the point. You give us a few hours upfront, we handle everything else." |

---

## NOTES FOR THE CALLER

- **Posture:** operator-to-operator. Not consultant-to-prospect.
- **Numbers cited above** ("10–15 hours/week", "by month three") match the homepage and `/comparison` page — keep them in lockstep when copy changes.
- **The "20 minute workflow audit" CTA** is the soft close. Hard ask is the discovery call (`/book`). The audit is optional — only use it if a prospect resists booking.
- **What we don't say on the call:** specific tooling (LangGraph, Anthropic, etc.), pricing tier names, our headcount. Save it for the kickoff once they've signed.
- **If they ask about the refund:** "30 days. If your first agent isn't live by day 30, full refund — no fine print, no partial payment, no questions." That answer should be one beat, then move on. Don't sell it twice.
