## The 5-minute version (founder interview / About page)

### Why I built Ramped

I've been an operator for over a decade. Founded Xtractor Depot in 2016, grew it into a multi-industry supplier of precision lab equipment. Our customers ranged from small cannabis labs to SpaceX and Lucid Motors.

Running a fast-scaling physical-goods business is mostly two things: sales (which scales with relationships and ICP fit) and operations (which scales linearly with hire-and-train). The first is fun. The second is a tax on growth.

We hired our first VA in 2019. By 2024 we had four. The ops bill was $14,400/mo, and three of them were doing the same email, every day, for different reasons.

I tried what every operator tries:
- **AI tools** — Lindy, Zapier AI, Gumloop. They worked for a weekend, broke when our ERP updated, and we fell back to manual. None of us had time to maintain them.
- **Consultants** — got two beautiful PowerPoints and a six-figure invoice. No working software.
- **Agencies** — built half a thing, billed monthly, churned when we asked for a fix.

The model that didn't exist: someone who deploys AI agents in your business AND runs them ongoing. Same shape as your bookkeeper or your fractional CFO. Not a tool. Not a consultant. A department, on retainer.

So I started building it.

### The first agent

Our first agent shipped in October 2024. It read inbound quote requests in our Gmail, cross-referenced our ERP for SKU pricing and stock levels, drafted a quote, and posted it to a Slack channel for one-touch approval.

In the first month, it handled 312 quote requests. Average response time dropped from 4.2 hours to 14 seconds. We caught 11 errors that would have shipped wrong (mostly stale shipping addresses — our ops team had been doing that math in their heads).

Two things happened next:

1. We laid off two of our four VAs (kindly — gave severance, helped with placements). The two we kept got promoted to managers of the AI agents.

2. Three of our supplier-side friends asked if we'd build the same thing for them. That was the moment Ramped became a separate company.

### What Ramped actually is

We deploy 3-7 AI agents in your business, in 30 days, on a flat monthly retainer ($2,500/mo Starter, $5,000/mo Growth, custom for Enterprise). The agents live in your existing stack — Slack, HubSpot, NetSuite, QuickBooks, whatever you use. We build them. We monitor them. We fix them when third-party APIs change.

You don't write any code. You don't hire any AI engineers. You approve drafts before they go out, you watch the work happen in your Slack, and once a month we do a strategy call to scope the next agents.

If we miss the 30-day live deadline, we refund the entire onboarding fee. No questions, no edge cases.

### Why we're different

Most AI companies sell software. We sell a department.

Software vendors win when you sign the contract. They lose when you stop using the product, but you still keep paying.

Departments win when the work gets done. If the bot misses an email, that's our fire to put out — not yours.

That's what "we own the outcome" actually means. It's the difference between a tool you forgot you were paying for and a teammate who shows up Monday morning.

### Where we're going

Ramped is currently 5 customers, 4 founding-team members. Goal for the next 12 months: 50 customers, $250K MRR, every customer renewing past month 6. Goal for 24 months: a partnership with a major MSP or BPO so they can offer "AI department" alongside their managed services.

We're not building a product company. We're building an operating model. And the operating model is: someone other than the customer owns the AI.

That's worth a lot to a lot of people. We'll find out exactly how much.

— Andrew
