## Content production pipeline

Every Monday morning:
1. **Review** last week's metrics. What landed? What didn't?
2. **Plan** the week's posts using this calendar
3. **Draft** all posts for the week (in `LINKEDIN-POSTS.md`, `TWITTER-X-POSTS.md`, etc.)
4. **Schedule** in Buffer / Hootsuite / native scheduler
5. **Block 1 hour** for engagement (replying to comments) on each posting day

Every Friday afternoon:
1. **Pull** the week's metrics: views, reactions, comments, replies, follows
2. **Save** the top 2 performing posts in `WINNING-POSTS.md` with notes on why
3. **Adjust** next week's calendar based on what worked

## What to post when something happens

| Event | Channel response |
|---|---|
| New customer signed | LinkedIn announcement (with permission), X note, email mention next newsletter |
| Customer hits a milestone | Case study post, IG reel, founder LinkedIn story |
| New agent shipped | Build-in-public post on LinkedIn + X thread |
| Competitor news | React fast on X (within 4 hrs); LinkedIn post within 24 hrs if it's relevant |
| Industry news that supports our thesis | Same-day X post; LinkedIn within 48 hrs |
| Won a deal | Founder LinkedIn note with details abstracted (no naming the deal until customer signs off) |
| Lost a deal to a competitor | Internal post-mortem only. Don't post about losses publicly. |

## Topical hooks (always-on)

These are evergreen post angles. Drop them in any week:

1. "We deployed for [Customer]" (with permission)
2. "What [vertical] looks like 60 days into Ramped"
3. "The 5 workflows we automate first at every customer"
4. "How we know an AI agent is broken before the customer does"
5. "The math that makes AI departments cheaper than VAs"
6. "Why we say no to [common request]" (positioning)
7. "What I learned shipping agent #50"
8. "A question I got on a sales call this week"

## What NOT to post

- Anything about layoffs / firings (politically risky)
- Generic AI news commentary (too saturated; only react if directly relevant)
- Self-congratulatory posts ("excited to announce...")
- Pricing changes without strategic context
- Speculation on competitors' strategies
- Anything off-brand (politics, religion, controversy unrelated to ops/AI)
