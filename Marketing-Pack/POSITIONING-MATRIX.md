## The map

There are three axes that matter in the AI-agent market in 2026:

1. **Self-serve ↔ Done-for-you** (who runs it)
2. **Tools ↔ Department** (what you're buying)
3. **Tech-savvy ↔ Operator** (who buys it)

Most companies cluster in the upper-left quadrant: self-serve tools sold to tech-savvy buyers. Ramped is alone in the lower-right: done-for-you department sold to operators.

## Direct competitor profiles

### Viktor (getviktor.com)

**Positioning:** "Not a tool. A hire." — autonomous AI coworker that lives in Slack/Teams, executes real tasks, 3,000+ tool integrations.

**Where they win:** Self-directed power users who want to work with an AI in chat. Strong product. Cool brand. Cheeky tone.

**Where they lose:** Operators who don't have time to direct an AI all day. The bot is reactive — you have to talk to it.

**Our angle:** "Viktor is excellent for self-directed work — you talk to it, it does. We're the opposite: we deploy AI agents that run autonomously in your business, and we maintain them on your behalf. Different go-to-market: Viktor sells to AI-savvy operators; we sell to CEOs who want someone else to own the outcome."

**Don't say:** Viktor is bad. They're not. We just serve a different buyer.

---

### Lindy (lindy.ai)

**Positioning:** "Your first AI employee" — agent platform for marketing, sales, ops automation. 200+ apps integrated.

**Where they win:** Marketers and growth teams who can write prompts and want self-serve. Strong product polish, great onboarding.

**Where they lose:** When the agent breaks 6 weeks in. They're a tool company; maintenance is on you.

**Our angle:** "Lindy is a great platform if you have someone in-house who'll maintain agents long-term. We're for the case where you don't — we deploy and run agents on your existing stack, on a flat retainer."

**Don't say:** Lindy is hard to use. It isn't. We're targeting buyers who don't want to use AI builders at all.

---

### Gumloop (gumloop.com)

**Positioning:** Visual AI workflow builder, drag-and-drop. "Build agents in minutes."

**Where they win:** Power users who like Zapier-style visual builders, especially for prototypes.

**Where they lose:** Production reliability. Visual workflow tools get fragile at scale.

**Our angle:** "Gumloop is great for prototyping and one-off automations. We're for production-grade agents that run inside an operating business — different reliability bar."

---

### Zapier AI (zapier.com)

**Positioning:** Add AI to existing Zapier workflows. Reach: enormous (Zapier has millions of users).

**Where they win:** Existing Zapier customers who want to add AI to their automations cheaply.

**Where they lose:** Workflow complexity ceiling. Zapier is great for simple A-to-B; struggles with multi-step reasoning.

**Our angle:** "Zapier AI is a great add-on to Zapier. If your workflows are simple A-to-B, perfect. We're for workflows that need actual judgment — qualifying leads, drafting personalized replies, cross-referencing systems."

---

### UiPath / Automation Anywhere

**Positioning:** Enterprise RPA + AI. Sold via long enterprise cycles.

**Where they win:** $100M+ enterprises with complex compliance requirements and 6-month procurement cycles.

**Where they lose:** SMBs and mid-market. Way too heavy, too expensive, too slow.

**Our angle:** "UiPath is the Oracle of automation. We're the Stripe — modern, fast, fits in 30 days, priced for SMB/mid-market."

---

### Adept AI / SmythOS / WhiteHorse

**Positioning:** AI agent platforms with various specializations.

**Where they win:** Specific verticals or niches where their agents are pre-trained.

**Where they lose:** General-purpose ops automation across multiple workflows.

**Our angle:** "Specialized AI tools work great when you have ONE workflow to automate. We work great when you want a department running across 5+ workflows on a single retainer."

## Substrate threats (not direct competitors)

### Claude / ChatGPT / Cowork

**The threat:** Anthropic's Cowork (Claude desktop app) and OpenAI's ChatGPT Agent are increasingly capable. Eventually, anyone can describe a task and an agent does it.

**Why we're still safe (for 2-3 years):**
1. **Integration depth** — agents only work if they're plugged into the customer's actual stack (NetSuite, HubSpot, etc.). Generic chat agents don't integrate.
2. **Maintenance** — when third-party APIs change, agents break silently. We monitor and fix; ChatGPT Agent doesn't.
3. **Approval flow** — production B2B requires human approval for customer-facing actions. Generic agents don't have this UX.
4. **Operating model** — even when AI gets cheaper, the cost of running it stays. Operators still need someone to own outcomes.

**Long-term play:** Position as the *operator* of AI infrastructure, not the *provider* of AI. Same model as managed cloud services — even when AWS got cheap, MSPs still own the relationship.

---

### In-house AI engineering hires

**The threat:** A $250K AI engineer can build what we ship.

**Why we're still safe (for SMBs):**
1. **Cost** — $250K all-in, plus 2-3 month ramp. Our Growth tier is $5K/mo.
2. **Hiring difficulty** — senior AI engineers are hard to recruit; SMBs lose to FAANG.
3. **Singular dependency** — if your AI engineer quits, you lose the institutional knowledge. With us, the work stays in our company.

**Where this threat WINS:** $50M+ ARR companies with dedicated engineering. They should hire in-house. We acknowledge this on sales calls.

## Differentiation talking points

When asked "why you over [competitor]," lead with one of these:

1. **Done-for-you operating model** (not a tool, a service)
2. **30-day go-live guarantee** (no other competitor offers an SLA this concrete)
3. **Same monthly cost as one VA** (anchor against staffing, not against software)
4. **Approval-by-default safety** (every customer-facing action goes through human review)
5. **We maintain it** (when APIs change, we get paged, not you)

In ranking order of effectiveness on operator buyers:
1 > 2 > 3 > 5 > 4

## What to NEVER claim

1. "We're the only ones who do this" — false, we have direct competitors (Viktor sometimes overlaps)
2. "Our AI is smarter" — model quality is roughly equivalent across vendors using Claude/GPT
3. "We're cheaper than competitors" — only true vs UiPath; Viktor is roughly the same price for fewer seats
4. "We integrate with everything" — we don't. We integrate with what we've built, which is the major-stack tools
5. "AI replaces your team" — politically risky, alienates operators who care about their people
