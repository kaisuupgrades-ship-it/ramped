# Email Outbound — Ramped AI

4 cold-email sequences targeted by buyer pain. Each sequence is 4 emails over 14 days. All written in operator voice — no corporate filler, no "I hope this finds you well", no follow-up apologies.

## Targeting

| Sequence | Target | ICP signal |
|---|---|---|
| A | Operations pain | $5M-$50M revenue, 10-100 employees, hires ops VAs, posts about "process improvement" |
| B | VA replacement | Job postings for "Operations VA", "Customer Service Rep", or 3+ existing VAs on LinkedIn |
| C | AI fatigue | Tried Zapier/Lindy/Gumloop and posted about it not working, or stopped posting after a year of trying |
| D | Post-funding | Series A/B in last 6 months, hiring ops + customer success aggressively |

## Subject line rules

- Under 50 chars (mobile preview)
- No emojis
- No "Quick question" (filtered as spam by every CEO inbox)
- Personalize when possible (specific to their company)

## Things to NEVER do in cold email

1. Use "Hope this finds you well" or any corporate opener
2. Apologize for following up
3. Send more than 4 emails
4. Use first-name-only subject lines (looks like a phishing attempt)
5. Include attachments (kills deliverability)
6. Send on weekends (lower open + reply rates)
7. Send between 10-11am Eastern (saturated)
8. Use "we" before establishing what we are (sounds presumptuous)

## A/B testing

For each sequence, run 2 variants of subject line + opening:
- Variant 1: As written above
- Variant 2: Personal anecdote opener (e.g., "I sent the wrong shipping address last September..." for Sequence A)

Run for 100 sends each. Pick winner. Iterate.
