# Objection Responses — Ramped AI Sales Playbook Supplement

15 common objections + battle-tested responses. Use these in discovery calls, demos, and email follow-ups. Every response leads with empathy ("makes sense"), reframes the assumption, and ends with a forward question or concrete next step.

---

## 1. Pricing objections

### "It's too expensive"

**The reframe:** It's the same monthly cost as one ops VA, but with 24/7 coverage and no quitting.

**The response:**
"Makes sense to ask. Quick math: if you've ever had a part-time VA at $25/hr × 80 hrs = $2,000/mo, that's right around our Starter tier. Difference is the VA quits in 9 months on average and you spend 3 weeks training the next one. The agent doesn't quit and gets *better* over time as we tune it. So the question isn't really 'is $2,500/mo expensive' — it's 'what's it worth to never train the same role again.' Want me to show you a couple of customers who pulled this trade-off?"

### "Can we do month-to-month?"

**The reframe:** Yes, no contract — but the 30-day go-live makes the first month a wash.

**The response:**
"Fully month-to-month, no contract. Heads up though: the first month is mostly setup and onboarding — that's why we have the 30-day live deadline. Most customers get the real value starting month 2. If you want to test cheap, we can scope a 60-day pilot at the Starter tier — 30 days to live, 30 days to validate the value, then decide."

### "We don't have budget right now"

**The reframe:** When does budget free up — and what would it take to fund this?

**The response:**
"Got it. Quick question — is the budget gap because the project isn't a priority, or because the calendar isn't right? If it's calendar (e.g., you've got existing ops headcount you're not ready to redirect yet), let's chat in 60 days. If it's priority, what would have to be true for this to move up?"

## 2. Skepticism objections

### "We tried AI tools before and they didn't work"

**The reframe:** That's exactly the problem we exist to solve — we're the maintainer, not just the builder.

**The response:**
"You and 80% of the operators we talk to. The pattern is always the same — built it on Lindy/Zapier/Gumloop, worked for 3 weeks, broke when something updated, fell back to manual. That's not an AI problem, that's an ownership problem. Tools without a maintainer break. We're the maintainer. When HubSpot updates an API and breaks the agent, we get paged, not you. Want me to show you the SLA we run on this?"

### "How do I know it'll actually work for our specific business?"

**The reframe:** We're 30 days in or it's free. Lowest-risk test in the AI category.

**The response:**
"Best answer: the 30-day go-live guarantee. If we don't have a working agent in your stack within 30 days of kickoff, you don't pay the onboarding fee. So worst case: you're out 30 days of calendar time. We've never refunded — but the offer is unconditional. Want me to walk through the discovery process so you can see where we'd start?"

### "Can it really handle our complexity?"

**The reframe:** We start narrow — one workflow at a time — then scale.

**The response:**
"Probably. But we don't try to handle all your complexity in week 1. The deployment goes one workflow at a time — pick the highest-ROI repetitive task, automate that, prove it, then layer the next one. By month 3 you've usually got 4-7 agents running across different functions. We never bite off more than you can validate in 30 days."

### "What if the AI makes a mistake?"

**The reframe:** Approval-by-default — every customer-facing action goes through human review.

**The response:**
"Built into the design. Every email, message, or external action the agent generates goes through your approval before it's sent. You see it in Slack, click 'Send' or 'Edit', that's it. The agent never sends to a customer without your one-click sign-off. The only autonomous actions are read operations and internal Slack posts — nothing that touches your reputation."

## 3. Competitive objections

### "How are you different from Lindy / Zapier / Gumloop?"

**The reframe:** They're tools. We're a department. You don't hire a hammer.

**The response:**
"Those are great tools. They're sold to operators with a CTO mindset — people who'll build it themselves and maintain it. You're an operator, not a developer. We deploy the same kind of agents but we run them. So when something breaks, you don't fix it — we do. That's the core difference between a tool and a department. Same difference as Excel vs your accountant."

### "How are you different from Viktor?"

**The reframe:** Viktor is a self-serve AI coworker. Ramped is a managed AI implementation partner.

**The response:**
"Viktor is excellent for self-directed work — you talk to it, it does. Different model from ours. We're a managed service — we build, deploy, and maintain the agents on your behalf. Two different go-to-markets: Viktor sells to AI-savvy operators; we sell to CEOs who want someone else to own the outcome. If you want to direct the AI yourself, Viktor is great. If you want a department running, we're it."

### "How are you different from a regular agency?"

**The reframe:** Agencies sell projects. We sell ongoing operations.

**The response:**
"Agencies build things and hand them off. We deploy AND run the agents on a flat monthly retainer — same model as your bookkeeper or your fractional CFO. The work doesn't end when the agent goes live. We monitor it, fix it, tune it, and add new agents over time. That's why we charge monthly, not per-project."

### "Can we just hire an in-house AI engineer?"

**The reframe:** You can. The total cost is 4-5× ours, and you still need someone to run them.

**The response:**
"You can absolutely. Ballpark: a senior AI engineer is $250K all-in, plus 2-3 months to ramp on your business. Our Growth tier is $5K/mo. Same shape of work, different operating model. The real question is: do you want this to be a hiring problem or a vendor problem? The right answer depends on your scale. If you're at $50M+ ARR with a clear AI-native product roadmap, hire in-house. If you're sub-$50M and AI is supporting ops/sales/CS, we're the better play."

## 4. Process objections

### "We need approvals before we can start"

**The reframe:** We can start the discovery call now without commitment — that's where the actual value is.

**The response:**
"Totally fair. Two paths: (1) we do the 30-min discovery call now, you walk away with a roadmap regardless of approvals — that's free and useful even if Ramped isn't a fit. (2) you take the call back to your team and we kick off when timing's right. Either way the discovery is the asset. Which works for you?"

### "Send me a deck and I'll review with my team"

**The reframe:** Decks aren't useful — a 30-min call is.

**The response:**
"Honest answer — decks aren't really how we pitch. The thing that's actually compelling is the discovery call where we map your specific workflows. Even if you don't move forward, you walk away with a list of every place AI could plug in at your company. Worth 30 min for that audit alone — happy to send a calendar link if you want to skip ahead."

### "How long does deployment really take?"

**The reframe:** 30 days from contract to first agent live, no exceptions.

**The response:**
"30 days from contract to first agent live in production. Day 1: discovery. Day 7: roadmap. Day 14: first agent in your stack. Day 21: QA. Day 30: live with monitoring. That's our SLA, not aspiration. We've held it on every customer."

### "Who owns the IP / data / agents?"

**The reframe:** You own everything. We don't lock in.

**The response:**
"You own all the data, all the agent configurations, all the workflows. If you stop working with us, we hand off the agents — they keep running. We don't lock you in. The thing you're paying for is us running them, not the agents themselves. Stop running them yourself? Sure. We'll write up a transition plan."

## 5. Internal alignment objections

### "I need to talk to my COO/CFO/CTO"

**The reframe:** Great — let's bring them in. We'll make sure they get what they need.

**The response:**
"Smart. Want to do a 3-way call so we can address their specific questions in real time? That moves faster than email tag. I can pull together the financial projections for the CFO, the security/IT story for the CTO, the operating model for the COO — all in one 30-min slot."

### "We're going to wait until [next quarter]"

**The reframe:** What changes next quarter that we should plan around?

**The response:**
"Got it. Two questions: (1) What changes next quarter — is it a budget cycle, a planning offsite, a hire? (2) If something hits the ROI threshold before then, would you accelerate? I'm asking because waiting 90 days = 90 days of compounding cost on the workflows we'd be automating. Sometimes that math wins, sometimes it doesn't. Just want to know which it is for you."

### "We need to get more buy-in internally"

**The reframe:** Happy to help — what specifically do they need to see?

**The response:**
"What's the specific concern from the team? If it's capacity, I can do a workshop with them. If it's whether AI is real, I can show 3 customers running agents this week. If it's whether we're trustworthy as a partner, references happy to chat. What's the actual gap?"

## What NEVER to do

1. **Argue.** If they say no, ask why and listen.
2. **Promise things we can't deliver.** "Yes, the AI will solve that" without scoping = future problem.
3. **Drop the price first.** If you discount before they've quantified value, you've signaled price was the issue.
4. **Send pre-written email replies.** Always personalize the lead-in.
5. **Use "what would it take" as a closing pressure.** It's manipulative.

---

## What to ALWAYS do

1. **Say "makes sense"** before you reframe. Disarms.
2. **Ask one specific follow-up question.** Keeps them talking.
3. **Quantify the loss of inaction.** "Each week of waiting = ~$X of unautomated cost."
4. **Offer the discovery call as a useful free asset** even if they don't sign.
5. **Send a recap email within 4 hours** of every call with concrete next steps.

## Call structure (30-min discovery)

| Minutes | Section | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| 0-2 | Intro / context | Set tone, agenda |
| 2-12 | Their workflow audit | List 5-10 manual workflows |
| 12-22 | Show 1-2 relevant customer cases | Specificity > generality |
| 22-27 | Scope a 30-day plan | First 1-2 agents to ship |
| 27-30 | Close: confirm next step | Either contract or follow-up date |

Don't skip the workflow audit. Even prospects who don't sign say "the discovery call alone was worth it."
